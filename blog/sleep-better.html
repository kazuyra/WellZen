How Teens Can Improve Sleep Quality

Teenage brains need 8–10 hours of sleep — but school, screens, and stress make that tough. Here’s a science-based guide to sleeping better without changing your whole life.

Why sleep matters

Sleep is one of the most powerful tools for memory, focus, mood, and long-term health.

  • Better school performance
  • Improved mental health
  • Stronger immune system
  • More energy and motivation

Quick habits that work

  • Consistent wake time — even weekends
  • Avoid screens 60 minutes before bed
  • No caffeine after lunchtime
  • Dark, cool, quiet bedroom
  • Daily sunlight exposure (morning is best)

Common sleep myths

✅ “I can catch up on weekends” — partial truth, but irregular patterns still harm focus
❌ “I need 6 hours, I'm fine” — most teens are sleep deprived and don’t realize it

When to get help

If you regularly can't sleep, feel exhausted at school, or have anxiety around sleep, talk to a doctor, school counselor, or trusted adult.

Sources: CDC, NIH, AAP
